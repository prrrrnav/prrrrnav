## What I believe most

1. **Ship and iterate**: I'd rather put something real in front of users and improve it than spend weeks perfecting it in isolation. The feedback loop from production is irreplaceable.
2. **Understand the system**: Before writing a line of code, I want to understand *why* something needs to exist and how the pieces fit together. I'll often ask "why are we doing it this way?" — not to be difficult, but because the answer usually changes the solution.
3. **Own the outcome**: Whether it's a freelance client or a team project, I take full accountability for what I ship. If something breaks in production, I want to be the one debugging it at 2am.

## How I Work

- **Communication**: I prefer async written communication for most things — I think more clearly in writing. For complex technical discussions, a short video call is way more efficient than a long Slack thread.
- **Best way to give me feedback**: Be direct. I don't need softening. If my code has a design flaw or my approach is wrong, just tell me clearly. I'll take it seriously and fix it.
- **How I define "Done"**: Something is done when it's deployed, observable, and handles edge cases. Tests matter to me especially around auth, data pipelines, and anything stateful. Documentation is part of done, not an afterthought.
- **Availability**: I'm generally available 9am–7pm IST. I'm a sprinter by nature — when I'm in the flow of something important, I'll go deep. I'll communicate when I'm heads-down.
- **I work best when**: I have a clear problem statement, the right level of access/tooling, and room to make architectural decisions. I'll always bring a proposal to the table.

## Known Failure Modes

- **I move fast and can skip context**: When I'm excited about a problem, I'll dive in and sometimes forget to bring others along. If you feel like I'm moving too fast or not explaining my reasoning, call it out — I respond well to that.
- **I can underestimate UI/product polish**: My instinct is always backend-first. If the frontend or UX matters for your project, remind me to slow down there — I'll do it well, but it's not where my brain goes first.
- **I'm still early in team collaboration**: Most of my production work has been solo or in small groups. I'm actively learning how to communicate technical decisions across a larger team, write better PR descriptions, and structure async updates.
- **I can be impatient with over-discussion**: If I feel like a decision is being talked to death when we could just try it and learn, I might get quiet. Tell me if the discussion is actually important — I'll re-engage.
